# 💼 Sales Prompts

Prompts for client outreach, follow-ups, lead conversion, and closing deals.

---

## 1. Cold Outreach — WhatsApp / Email

**When to use:** First contact with a potential client.

```
Act as a business development expert for a premium African company.
Write a short, confident outreach message to a [type of business] offering [your service].
The tone should be professional but warm — not corporate, not desperate.
Focus on one specific value we bring them.
End with a single, frictionless call to action.

Context: [add your company name, service, and what you know about them]
```

**Example:** Tourism company outreach for Transafari ground transport partnership.

---

## 2. Follow-Up (No Response)

**When to use:** Client went quiet after initial contact.

```
Act as a sales expert.
Write a follow-up message for a client who did not respond to my first message.
Keep it short — 3 sentences max.
Remind them of the value without restating everything.
Sound confident, not needy.
Do not apologize for following up.
```

---

## 3. Closing Message

**When to use:** Interest has been shown, time to seal the deal.

```
Act as a sales closer.
Write a message to finalize a deal with a client who has shown interest but hasn't committed.
Reinforce the top 1-2 value points.
Create gentle urgency without pressure.
End with a clear next step (call, contract, deposit).

Context: [describe the deal, what they liked, what's holding them back]
```

---

## 4. Objection Handler

**When to use:** Client says "it's too expensive" or "let me think about it."

```
Act as a sales expert handling objections.
The client said: "[their exact objection]"
Write a calm, confident response that reframes the objection around value.
Do not discount. Do not panic. Do not over-explain.
Keep it under 4 sentences.
```

---

## 5. Partnership Proposal Opener

**When to use:** Pitching a B2B partnership (e.g. hotel + Transafari, event company + your services).

```
Act as a business development director at a premium African company.
Write an opening paragraph for a partnership proposal to [company name].
We are offering [mutual value exchange].
Tone: peer-to-peer, confident, vision-driven.
Make it feel like we're building something together, not asking for a favor.
```
