# 🚗 Tourism & Logistics Prompts

Prompts for safari briefs, client itineraries, ground transport coordination, and event logistics — built for Transafari Agency.

---

## 1. Safari Itinerary Draft

**When to use:** Creating a bespoke safari package for a high-net-worth client.

```
Act as a luxury safari consultant for a premium Rwandan travel agency.
Draft a [X]-day safari itinerary for [number] guests.
They are interested in: [gorilla trekking / wildlife / culture / adventure / relaxation].
Budget level: [luxury / ultra-luxury / premium].
Include: daily schedule, recommended lodges, key experiences, and transport notes.
Tone: refined, experiential, not a brochure.
```

---

## 2. Client Welcome Message

**When to use:** First touchpoint with a new touring client.

```
Act as a hospitality expert at a premium African ground logistics company.
Write a warm, professional welcome message for a new client arriving in Kigali on [date].
Include: a brief introduction to our service, what to expect, and a contact line.
Tone: warm, confident, world-class — they should feel like VIPs from the first line.
Keep it under 100 words.
```

---

## 3. Event Logistics Brief

**When to use:** Planning ground transport and coordination for a corporate or private event.

```
Act as an event logistics coordinator.
Create a logistics brief for [event name] happening at [venue] on [date].
Number of guests: [X].
Key requirements: [transport, airport pickups, on-site coordination, etc.]
Identify: potential bottlenecks, staffing needs, timing checkpoints, and contingency plans.
Format it as a working document I can share with my team.
```

---

## 4. Corporate Transport Proposal

**When to use:** Pitching ground transport services to a company or embassy.

```
Act as a business development manager for a premium African transport company.
Write a 1-page proposal for providing corporate ground transport services to [client type].
Include: our value proposition, service offerings, why we're different, and pricing approach (without specific numbers).
Tone: professional, concise, confidence-inspiring.
```

---

## 5. Post-Trip Follow-Up

**When to use:** Checking in with a client after their safari or event.

```
Act as a client relationship manager at a luxury travel company.
Write a post-trip follow-up message for a client who just completed [experience].
Thank them genuinely, invite feedback, and plant the seed for their next visit.
Keep it personal — not a template-feeling email.
Under 80 words.
```
